Reconfigurable All-Optical Multilogic Gate (XOR, AND, OR) Based on Cross-Phase Modulation in Highly Nonlinear Fiber

This project focuses on the design and simulation of a high-speed, reconfigurable all-optical logic gate capable of performing XOR, AND, and OR operations using the nonlinear phenomenon of Cross-Phase Modulation (XPM) in Highly Nonlinear Fiber (HNLF). Unlike conventional semiconductor optical amplifier (SOA)-based logic gates, this system maintains signal processing entirely in the optical domain, eliminating optical-electrical-optical (O-E-O) conversion, thereby reducing delay, complexity, and power consumption.

The working principle is based on the interaction between high-power pump pulses and low-power probe pulses inside a 1 km HNLF. Through XPM, the pump signal induces a phase shift in the probe signal, resulting in controlled wavelength shifts (±0.078 nm). Band-Pass Filters (BPFs) are strategically tuned to select either the original or shifted wavelengths to realize different logical operations. The system was modeled and simulated in OptiSystem at a bit rate of 10 Gb/s using NRZ pulse generators.

Experimental results demonstrate successful implementation of XOR, AND, and OR logic functions with clear waveform outputs and acceptable extinction ratios, confirming the feasibility of high-speed all-optical signal processing for future optical communication networks.
